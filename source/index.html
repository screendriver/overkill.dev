Testing is overkill.

Why test anything? Ship first. Think never. Hope forever.

Plot Twist

That opening line is satire. "Testing is overkill" is the fastest way to build brittle systems, panic-driven releases, and bug archeology as a full-time job.

The real overkill is debugging production at 3:17 AM because we skipped ten minutes of design and ten lines of tests.

Classic advice: "Why write tests when your commit message is descriptive?"

Myths versus reality

  • Myth: Tests slow delivery

    Reality: rework slows delivery. Good tests are speed insurance with receipts.

  • Myth: TDD is ceremony

    Reality: tiny feedback loops are cheaper than giant post-release surprises.

  • Myth: Quality is for perfectionists

    Reality: quality is for teams that like sleeping during the night.
